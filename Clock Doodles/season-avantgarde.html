The Gradient

The same four seasonal washes as the Hyper Modern page — because the gradient is the canvas, not the decoration. What changes is everything placed on top of it. With this face the gradient is allowed to be more aggressive: the typography is sparse enough that the eye travels into the colour rather than resting on the numbers. Autumn in particular reads as almost abstract — the warm burn in the lower-right becomes its own subject.

Avant-Garde Face

The clock is deconstructed. Hours are so large they bleed off the left edge — you read the number the way you read a cropped photograph, inferring the whole from the visible fragment. A razor vertical rule bisects the card: everything left of it is mass, everything right is negative space. Minutes live rotated 90° up the right margin, small and clinical, like a label on a specimen jar. The seconds bar is not a circle or a sweep — it is a hairline that fills left to right, industrial and exact. A ghost season letterform haunts the right quadrant at near-zero opacity: texture, not content. The week strip is dots and numbers with no containers, no cards, no backgrounds — just marks on a surface.

Design Logic

Deconstructed time: Hours are a raw typographic mass cropped at a vertical rule — the number is so large it bleeds off the left edge. Minutes live rotated 90° on the right margin, subordinate and clinical. Hierarchy through scale: every element is a different size, weight, and opacity — layered depth without boxes or cards. Seconds bar: a hairline track sweeps once per minute — not a circle, not a countdown, just a quiet pulse. Season glyph: a ghost letterform fills the right quadrant at near-zero opacity — texture, not information.
