A Clock for Claude UTC

The Background

No seasonal gradient here. The background shifts with the actual hour of day — a radial gradient whose two colours change every hour through a hand-tuned palette of 24 near-blacks. At dawn there is a faint rust warmth. At noon the canvas is neutral. At dusk it burns slightly amber. At midnight it cools toward blue-black. The change is slow enough that you never catch it moving. It is less a design choice than a reflex — a clock that tells you the time should probably feel like the time.

The Face

Font-weight 100 — the lightest available — because something important does not need to shout. The time is enormous but barely there, more like weather than an announcement. The colon blinks because it is the only animation and it earns its place: it is actually telling you something. Warm parchment ink on near-black rather than white on black, because white on black is a screen convention and this is meant to feel like reading. The entropy bar is the one element I would actually want on my own clock: not the seconds, not a sweep — the fraction of today already gone. It moves in real time and it is the most honest thing on the page. The thought line changes on the minute, not randomly. At any given minute it shows the same phrase. You could close the page and return and it would say what it should say.
