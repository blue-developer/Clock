The Gradient

Each season is a single diagonal wash — top-left to bottom-right at 150° — so the colour moves the way light moves: oblique, unhurried. Spring is a conversation between cool mint and warm blush, the palette of new leaves and petals existing at the same moment. Summer drives hard from deep navy sky down to a hot amber horizon — the full arc of a long day compressed into one frame. Autumn begins almost black and burns its way forward through sienna and pumpkin into dried-grass gold; it is the only gradient that earns its darkness. Winter is the inverse of summer — it starts in midnight cold and lifts toward ice and snow, a gradient that feels like looking up.

Hyper Modern Face

The time is ultra-light weight (font-weight 200) and enormous — it dominates without aggression. A hairline rule above it with a centred label keeps the composition anchored. AM/PM and seconds stack vertically beside the hours like a footnote: information present but subordinate. The date sits below as spaced small-caps, giving it editorial weight without competing. The week strip is frosted glass — it belongs to the same surface as the background, not a separate card. The overall mood is a luxury watch catalogue printed on the sky.
